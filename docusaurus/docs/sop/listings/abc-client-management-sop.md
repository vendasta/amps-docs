## Section 3: Primary Workflow: Claiming via API (Easy Claim)

Our API connection makes us a "Trusted Partner," which bypasses most standard verification steps. This is our default method.

### 3.1 Your Role (Team Member)

* **DO NOT** start by searching in the Apple Business Connect dashboard.
* Your primary action is to add the new client's information (Business Name, Address, Phone, Website, Categories) to our LocalSEO internal tool.
* This internal tool securely sends the location data to the Apple Business Connect API.
* The API automatically:
  * Finds or creates the listing.
  * Associates it with our "Broadly Teams" brand.
  * Applies a pre-verified or "easy claim" status.
* After a short processing time, you can log in to the ABC dashboard, navigate to the "Broadly Teams" brand, and see the new client location listed under the **Locations** tab, ready for management.

### 3.2 Client's Role (Business Owner)

* **Provide accurate information:** The client's only task for this method is to provide you with their exact, correct business information.
* **No verification needed:** They do not need to answer a phone call, check for a postcard, or provide a code. This is the main benefit of our API.

## Section 4: Manual Workflow (Backup & Edge Cases)

Use this only if the API method fails or for specific problem listings.

### 4.1 Adding a Brand New Business (Not in Apple Maps)

1. Inside the "Broadly Teams" brand, go to the **Locations** tab.
2. Click **Add a Location**.
3. Search for the business name and address. When no results appear, Apple will prompt you to **Add a new location**.
4. **Team Member Role:** Fill in all required information (Name, Address, Phone, Category) accurately.
5. **Client Role:** Apple will require manual verification. The client must be ready to:
   * Answer a verification phone call to the business number and provide you with the code, OR
   * Provide you with business documentation (utility bill, business license) that you can upload if Apple requests it.

### 4.2 Claiming an Existing, Unclaimed Business

1. Follow steps 1–2 from 4.1.
2. Search for the business. You will see it in the results.
3. Click the **Claim** button.
4. **Team Member Role:** Confirm the business details.
5. **Client Role:** Be on standby for the verification method (usually a phone call) as described in 4.1.

### 4.3 Requesting Access to an "Already Claimed" Listing

This is the most complex scenario. It means another person (the owner, a former employee, another agency) already has control.

1. Search for the business in the dashboard. You will see it is **Already Managed** or **Claimed**.

**Team Member Step 1 (Inquiry):** STOP and ask the client: *"Do you or anyone on your team (or a former agency) have access to an Apple Business Connect account?"*

* **If YES:** The client must log in and add you as a "Company Manager" or "Brand Manager" to their account. This is the cleanest method.
* **If NO (or "I don't know"):** Proceed to Step 2.

**Team Member Step 2 (Request):** Click the button to **Request Access.** Apple will not tell you who manages the listing. This sends an automated email to the unknown owner. They have 14 days to respond. Most of the time, this request is ignored.

**Team Member Step 3 (Escalation):** If the request fails or times out, you must contact Apple Business Connect support directly (see Section 5.2). This will become a manual "Appeal."

**Client Role:** The client must be ready to provide you with official business documents (e.g., utility bill, business license, certificate of incorporation) that prove they own the business at that address. You will need to submit this to Apple.

## Section 5: Advanced SOPs (Appeals & Duplicates)

### 5.1 Handling Duplicate Listings

**Problem:** You see two listings for the same client in Apple Maps.

**SOP:**

1. First, ensure you have claimed the "correct" one (the one with the right address/phone) using our API method.
2. Open the public Apple Maps app (on a Mac, iPhone, or iPad).
3. Find the incorrect duplicate listing.
4. Click the **...** button and select **Report an Issue**.
5. Select **This place is a duplicate** or **Information is incorrect**.
6. Follow the prompts to report it as a duplicate of your correct, managed listing. This is a manual review process by Apple.

### 5.2 Handling Appeals (Rejected Edits, Failed Claims)

**Problem:** Your claim (from Section 4.3) was rejected, or an important edit (like a name change) is not being approved.

**SOP:**

1. Log in to the ABC dashboard.
2. In the bottom-left corner, click **Help** (the ? icon).
3. Click **Contact Us** and follow the prompts to get in touch with the ABC support team.
4. Clearly state the issue (e.g., "We are the verified agency for [Client Name] and need to gain control of this listing from an unknown previous owner").
5. **Client Role:** Have the client's proof of ownership (business license, utility bill) ready to upload as a PDF. Apple support will almost always require this to resolve ownership disputes.

## Apple Business Connect: Claiming FAQs

### 1. Eligibility & Business Types

**Q: Does a business really need a physical location?**  
Yes, to have a "place card" on Apple Maps, the business must have a physical, verifiable address that customers can visit.

**Q: What about online-only or e-commerce businesses?**  
Based on late 2024 updates, online-only businesses can register with Apple Business Connect, but not for a Maps listing. They can register for brand features like "Branded Mail" and "Tap to Pay" branding. For your team's SOP, if the client is e-commerce only, they are not eligible for a Maps location claim.

**Q: What about Service Area Businesses (SABs) like plumbers or cleaners?**  
Apple Maps does not have a "hide address" feature (unlike Google). If the client is an SAB and uses a home address, that home address will be made public on Apple Maps if you claim it. The client must be okay with this. The alternative is to use a verifiable physical office address. If they have no public address, they are not eligible for a Maps listing.

**Q: Can we claim a "Coming Soon" location?**  
No. The location must be open and operational (or at least able to be verified) to be on Apple Maps. You can prepare the listing, but you cannot complete the verification and publish it until it is officially open.

### 2. The Verification Process

**Q: What are the main ways to verify a listing?**  
If our "Easy Claim" (API) method doesn't work, the manual options are: **Phone Call** (fastest—automated call to business number with 4-digit code); **Document Upload** (submit official business documents to Apple for manual review); **Domain Validation** (more for Company setup—verify you own the business's website domain via TXT record).

**Q: What documents does Apple accept for verification?**  
Apple is very strict. Documents must show the exact legal business name and exact physical address on the listing. Good examples: utility bill (electric, gas, water), business license, certificate of incorporation, tax documents. Bad examples: invoices, flyers, screenshots of a website.

**Q: The legal name is "XYZ Inc." but the client is "Known as Bob's Bots." What do I use?**  
You must use the legal name ("XYZ Inc.") for the verification and legal profile. Apple is much stricter than Google about DBAs. After you are verified, you can often adjust the display name to be more customer-friendly, but the legal entity name is required.

### 3. Common Problems & Timelines

**Q: Why did our "Easy Claim" (API) method not work?**  
This almost always means a data mismatch or a conflict: (1) **Data Mismatch**—client info in our system (phone, suite number, spelling) does not exactly match the data Apple has for that location; (2) **Listing is Already Managed**—the listing is already claimed by another user; the API cannot override this and you must use the manual "Request Access" workflow.

**Q: The listing is "Already Managed." What's the fastest way to fix this?**  
The fastest way is for the client to find the person who has access. Follow SOP 4.3: Ask the client first. If they do, they must log in and add you as a Company Manager. If they truly don't know, use the "Request Access" button (email to unknown owner; 14 days to respond). There is no way to speed this up until after it fails, at which point it becomes an "Appeal."

**Q: How long does manual verification take?**  
Phone call: instant. Document review: Apple states up to 5 business days, but it can sometimes be longer.

**Q: I created a brand new listing for a new client. How long until it shows on Apple Maps?**  
Set client expectations. After it's verified, it can take anywhere from 24 hours to two weeks for a brand new location to be fully indexed and start appearing in public Apple Maps searches.
