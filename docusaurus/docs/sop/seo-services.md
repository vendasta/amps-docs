## Perform a Competitive Analysis

**Timeline:** 1 to 2 Business Days.
**Responsibility:** SEO Specialist.

**Overview:** Competitive analysis involves identifying and analyzing the client's main competitors in the online space to understand their strategies and identify areas where you can outperform them. This includes looking at their keyword rankings, traffic sources, backlinks, content, and other relevant factors.

**Why It's Important:**  
 Competitive analysis helps you understand the SEO landscape and how a client's site compares to others. It reveals gaps and opportunities for growth, helping you refine your client's SEO strategy.

**How to Approach It:**
  **1. Identify Competitors:** Use SE Ranking’s Competitor Research module to input the client's domain and find competitors' ranking for similar keywords.
     * Ensure you're targeting the correct geo-location by using tools like Google and Bing to confirm the competition suggested by SE Ranking.



![](/img/sop-seo-services/01.png)
  **1. Analyze Organic Traffic:** Use SE Ranking’s Traffic Analysis module to analyze where competitors' traffic is coming from, including the specific keywords and traffic sources (search, social, etc.).



![](/img/sop-seo-services/02.png)

  **1. Backlink Analysis:** Use SE Ranking’s Backlink Checker module to see who is linking to a client's competitors and evaluate the quality of those backlinks.
     * Identify authoritative sites linking to the client's competitors and explore opportunities to gain backlinks from similar sources.



![](/img/sop-seo-services/03.png)

  **1. Content Analysis:** Review the types of content that are performing well for the client's competitors. Look at the content's structure, topics, and engagement metrics to find areas for improvement or replication.
     * Use SE Ranking's Competitive Research features to identify content gaps that you can fill with the client's content.

  **2. Note Weaknesses:** Identify weaknesses in the client's competitors’ strategies. Look for areas where they may be lacking, such as keyword targeting, content gaps, or low-quality backlinks. This helps you capitalize on these opportunities.

  **3. Compile Findings:** Gather all insights into a competitive analysis report that can be included in the Onboarding slide deck for the team or client.



Tools in SE Ranking to Use:
  * **Competitor Research:** For keyword, traffic, and backlink analysis.
  * **Traffic Analysis:** To track traffic sources for competitors.
  * **Backlink Checker:** To analyze competitor backlinks and quality.
  * **Competitive Research:** To identify content gaps and areas for improvement.

What is Website Auditing?  
 Website auditing involves assessing the current state of a client's website from a technical SEO perspective. This includes checking for technical issues that could hinder search engine crawling and indexing, as well as reviewing on-page SEO factors like content, titles, and meta descriptions.

**Why is it Important?**  
 Auditing is essential to uncover issues that are limiting a client's website’s performance. It ensures the website is accessible, fast, mobile-friendly, and optimized for search engines.

**How to Approach It:**
  **1. Technical SEO Check:** Focus on site speed , mobile-friendliness , crawl errors , structured data , and other critical technical SEO elements.
     * Tools like Google PageSpeed Insights (pagespeed.web.dev), GTMetrix , and Chrome Developer Tools can help assess performance. Use incognito mode to get accurate results.
     * **Mobile Check:** If possible, check the website on a smartphone or use a service like BrowserStack to verify mobile responsiveness.

## Research & Finalize Keyword Set

**Timeline:** 1 to 2 Business Days.
**Responsibility:** SEO Specialist.

**What is it?** Keyword research is the process of identifying the terms and phrases that users enter into search engines when looking for products, services, or information related to a business. This helps in understanding search intent and targeting the right keywords to drive relevant traffic.

**Why is it Important?** Keyword research is crucial because it ensures you target the right keywords that have high traffic potential while matching the user’s search intent. It helps in optimizing content to attract the right audience and improve rankings in search engine results.

**How to Approach Keyword Research:**
  **1. Brainstorm Initial Keyword Ideas:** Begin by brainstorming common terms and phrases related to the client's business, products, geo locations, and services.

  **2. Use Keyword Research Tools:** Leverage tools like SE Ranking , Google , Bing , and Google Keyword Planner to check the search volume, competitiveness, and relevance of keywords.

  **3. Analyze Competitors:** Review the keywords used by competitors in their content and observe their rankings. This helps in identifying keyword opportunities.

  **4. Consider Search Intent:** Evaluate the intent behind the keywords: Are users looking for information, services, or products? Ensure the keywords align with the user's search intent.

  **5. Use Long-Tail Keywords:** Incorporate longer, more specific keyword phrases (long-tail keywords). These often have lower competition and better user intent.

  **6. Group Keywords by Topic:** Organize keywords into categories or topics to better structure content and target multiple related search terms.



**How to Do Keyword Research with SE Ranking:**
  **1. Keyword Research Module:** Start with seed keywords related to the client's business and use SE Ranking’s Keyword Research module to find related search terms.

  **2. Analyze Search Volume and Difficulty:** Use the data on search volume , keyword difficulty , and competition from SE Ranking to determine which keywords are worth targeting.

  **3. Use Keyword Grouping Tool:** Organize keywords by category or topic using the Keyword Grouping Tool in SE Ranking. This helps structure the client's keyword strategy for better targeting.

  **4. Review Data from Google Search Console or Bing Webmaster Tools:** If connected, use Google Search Console or Bing Webmaster Tools to check the current search results and analyze which keywords are driving traffic to the client's website.

  **5. Finalize Keyword Set:** After analyzing the data, finalize the keyword set based on relevance, search volume, and competition.
     * Document the final list of keywords for future reference and include it in the consultation call deck.

## Establish Baseline Rank of Keyword Set

**Timeline:** 1 to 2 Business Days.
**Responsibility:** SEO Specialist.

**What is it?** Establishing a baseline rank involves recording the current rankings of selected keywords before any optimization efforts. This allows you to track changes over time and measure the effectiveness of client's SEO strategies.

**Why is it Important?** Setting a baseline rank is crucial for tracking SEO progress. It helps you understand where the client's keywords stand initially, so you can evaluate improvements after implementing changes.

**How to Approach It:**
  **1. Select Target Keywords:** Start by finalizing the list of keywords you want to track based on the keyword research you've conducted.

  **2. Track Initial Positions:** Before beginning any SEO work, use SE Ranking to check and document the current ranking of these keywords on both Google and Bing.

  **3. Record Baseline Rankings:** Save these initial rankings for future comparison. This data will serve as a reference point to evaluate the success of the client's SEO efforts.

  **4. Create a Baseline Rank Report:** Prepare a report that includes the baseline rankings for each target keyword. This will be used for future analysis and reporting.



**How to Do It with SE Ranking:**
  **1. Use the Rank Tracking Module:** Set up the keywords you want to track in SE Ranking’s Rank Tracking module.
  **2. Track Rankings:** SE Ranking will provide daily ranking updates for each keyword, which helps you monitor keyword movement over time.

  **3. Record Search Volume and Rank:** Record both search volume and rank for each keyword to gauge its current performance in search engines.

#### What are Backlinks?
Backlinks, also known as inbound links or incoming links , are links from one website to another. When website A links to website B, website B receives a backlink from website A. Backlinks are an important factor in SEO because they signal to search engines like Google that other websites trust and endorse the client's content.

In SEO, backlinks are considered a vote of confidence. The more quality backlinks you have, the more likely search engines will see the client's website as authoritative and trustworthy, which can positively affect the client's rankings.

### Why Are Backlinks Important for SEO?
  **1. Improved Search Engine Rankings:** Backlinks are one of the top-ranking factors for search engines like Google. Websites with more quality backlinks typically rank higher in search engine results pages (SERPs).
     * Backlinks help search engines assess the relevance , authority , and trustworthiness of a website, ultimately influencing its ranking.

  **2. Referral Traffic:** Backlinks not only improve SEO but also provide direct traffic to client's websites when users click on those links. A backlink from a high-traffic website can drive quality visitors to a client's site.

  **3. Building Domain Authority (DA):** Search engines assign a domain authority score to websites based on factors like the number and quality of backlinks. The more quality backlinks you acquire from authoritative websites, the higher the client's DA will be, which in turn helps you rank better.

  **4. Faster Indexing:** Search engines use backlinks to crawl the web and discover new pages. When a client's site has backlinks from authoritative sources, it’s easier for search engines to discover and index the client's new content.
