Tips for Effective Display Ads

Clear and Concise Messaging

* Your ad should communicate its main idea at a quick glance.

* Keep copy minimal and purposeful.

Strong Visual Hierarchy

* Use design to guide the viewer's eye: consider size, contrast, and placement.

* Highlight key info using larger fonts or accent colours.

High-Quality Imagery

* Only use high-resolution visuals.

* Avoid blurry or pixelated images to maintain a polished, professional feel.

Consistent Branding

* Stick to brand-approved fonts, colours, and styles.

* Consistency builds trust and recognition.

Limited Text

* Avoid text overload.

* If needed, keep it short and ensure it's legible at a glance.

Eye-Catching Colours

* Use bold or contrasting colors that align with your brand.

* Complementary hues can help your ad stand out visually.

Effective Use of White Space

* Don't overcrowd.

* Let your design "breathe" by spacing out elements purposefully.

Mobile-Friendly Design

* Many users view ads on mobile.

* Test across screen sizes to ensure readability and layout integrity.

Dynamic and Relevant Imagery

* Choose visuals your audience relates to.

* Ensure imagery supports your product, message, or CTA.

Call-to-Action (CTA)

* Use action verbs.

* Create urgency or clear value to drive clicks (e.g., "Get Started," "Shop Now").

Experiment with Animation

* Subtle animations can boost engagement.

* Avoid distractions—don't let animation overshadow the message.

Test Multiple Variations

* Run A/B tests for design elements (imagery, CTA, colors, layout).

* Optimize based on performance.

Adhere to Platform Guidelines

* Each platform (Google Display, Meta, etc.) has its own rules.

* Double-check dimensions, file sizes, and content restrictions.

Localized Imagery (If Applicable)

* Use culturally relevant or geographically tailored visuals to increase relatability.

Consider Your Audience:

* Know who you're designing for.

* Reflect their preferences and values in both imagery and tone.

Tips / Notes

* Display ad performance is highly iterative—plan for frequent testing and tweaks.

* When in doubt, keep it simple and user-focused.
